FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

My apps didn't unlock after I hit my goal

Sweat2Scroll re-checks your HealthKit progress roughly every 15 minutes in the background. To force a check, open the app — the dashboard refresh triggers an immediate policy evaluation. Also confirm Health access is granted in Settings → Privacy & Security → Health → Sweat2Scroll.

I deleted Sweat2Scroll but my apps are still blocked

iOS automatically removes all Screen Time shields an app has applied when that app is deleted, so your apps should unlock on their own. In rare cases an iOS system bug can leave a shield behind. If that happens:

  1. Reinstall Sweat2Scroll, sign in, and toggle the shield off — then delete the app again; or
  2. Go to Settings → Screen Time, turn Screen Time off and back on; or
  3. Restart your iPhone.

This is Apple system behavior, not data we control — no Sweat2Scroll servers are involved in shielding.

What is Break-Glass and how do I use it?

Break-Glass is an emergency override held by your accountability partner. Ask your partner for the current 6-digit code (it rotates and is single-use), tap Break-Glass in the app, and enter it. Your apps unlock for 15 minutes, and the event is recorded in your signed audit log.

My step / calorie data looks wrong or missing

Sweat2Scroll only mirrors what Apple Health reports. Check the Health app first — if the data is missing there, it will be missing here. An Apple Watch gives the most accurate active-calorie, HRV, and sleep data; iPhone-only tracking covers steps and some workouts.

How do I change my blocked apps or daily goal?

Profile tab → Daily calorie goal, or Profile tab → Restricted apps. Changes take effect immediately at the next policy evaluation.

Does my health data leave my phone?

No. All fitness data is processed in-memory on your device by an on-device policy engine. Nothing is sent to our servers — we don't have any. See the Privacy Policy for details.

How do I delete my account and data?

Sign out and delete the app to remove all local data. To erase your private CloudKit records (pairing contract, audit log), email us at coppersmith2222@gmail.com with the subject "Data deletion" — or manage iCloud data yourself in Settings → Apple ID → iCloud → Manage Account Storage.
